Marjorie Taylor Greene rap song gets hammered on social media: 'Is this a joke?'

August 31, 2024
post

WASHINGTON (TND) — In a new song from rapper Forgiato Blow, Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene, R-Ga., lays claim to the title of “MAGA’s MVP.”

The song, titled “MTG,” has earned nearly 100,000 views on YouTube since its release last week. Starring in the video alongside the rapper, whose real name is Kurt Jantz, Greene poses with props like an ornate throne and a sports car while he praises her political achievements.

“Marjorie, I really love what you do. Keep calling those RINOs out, no one does it better than you,” he raps. “A real business woman, AOC’s a featherweight. A southern belle, a little hood, watch her shake and bake.”

Commenters took to social media to poke fun at the song.

“There is nothing less hip hop than MTG. Any song about her should be over a banjo track,” a YouTube commenter said.

“Is this a joke?” another asked.

Sharing the video via Twitter, Greene said she hoped to bring awareness to her latest piece of legislation that would make it a felony to provide gender change therapies to minors.

“Thank you @forgiatoblow47 for brining [sic] awareness to my critical legislation, the Protect Children’s Innocence Act, in your new hit song MTG!” Greene wrote.

Jantz, the self-proclaimed “Mayor of Magaville,” is the pioneer of a new hip hop genre called “MAGA rap.” The genre features themes of conservative topics and support for former President Donald Trump. His song “Boycott Target,” previously reached the top spot on iTunes.

On his website, Jantz sells sweatshirts that read “Make America Trump Again” and “LGBT Let’s Go Brandon Tribe.” His previous works include titles such as “If You’re Reading This Trump Won,” “Jan6ers” and “Big Don and De$antis.”

YouTube creator and music critic Anthony Fantano once described people like Jantz as “disgusting, gut-churning filth babies” and likened him to a “husky, weird-looking meatball of a man.”
